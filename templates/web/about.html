Introduction:


Welcome to Georegistry.org, the geospatial health repository developed by the Earth Institute at Columbia University. What makes Georegistry.org special is that it is designed to result in high-quality, vetted, health information related to specific points-of-interest (POI) of value to development: health, education, water and sanitation. Information may be read or written from multiple sources, including mobile devices, spreadsheets, and websites.


In short, Georegistry makes it easy for governments and NGOs to share, visualize, and report information. Click here to see an example item in Google Maps. Click here to see a "raw response" from the Georegistry service in GeoJSON format.


Georegistry provides a standardized way to collect and store health data that is too often kept in spreadsheets in incompatible formats. If you are a software developer you may be interested in the developer documentation, or you may wish to request access to the application programming interface(API).




Georegistry Capabilities:


  • Store and search multiple spellings or languages for a single item
  • Duplicate detection
  • Flag items as "official" or government-verified
  • Historical storage (versioning) and search
  • Spreadsheet import tools
  • Developer Friendly: RESTful API serving GeoJSON, open source, Client Examples with Source Code
  • Connect via mobile devices, web, xforms, or other application
